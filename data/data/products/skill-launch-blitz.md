## What this skill does

A **21-platform submission playbook** your AI can execute autonomously to launch your product in one coordinated blitz across the platforms that actually move traffic.

**Without this skill:** you launch on ProductHunt, collapse from the effort, and forget the other 20 platforms. Or you submit everywhere at once and burn out.

**With this skill:** your AI schedules a 7-day launch sequence, drafts platform-native copy for each, monitors responses, and keeps you informed without eating your week.

---

## The 21 Platforms

### Tier 1 — Launch platforms (submit first)
1. **ProductHunt** — biggest visibility, Tuesday launch, 24-hour window
2. **Betalist** — pre-launch list, good for early waitlist
3. **Uneed** — curated weekly newsletter
4. **Fazier** — Reddit-like voting
5. **TrustMRR** — SaaS-focused MRR track
6. **TinyLaunch** — fast-moving new-product board

### Tier 2 — Content & community
7. **Hacker News (Show HN)** — high signal, unpredictable
8. **Indie Hackers** — founder-audience
9. **Reddit** — pick 1-3 subs that match your niche
10. **OpenAlternative** — if you're an OSS alternative
11. **PeerPush** — social-proof network
12. **Peerlist** — dev/designer audience

### Tier 3 — Directories & discovery
13. **SaaSHub** — SaaS directory
14. **SaaS Genius** — niche SaaS directory
15. **AlternativeTo** — when you're an alternative to X
16. **There's an AI for That (TAAFT)** — AI product directory
17. **DevHunt** — dev-focused
18. **Tiny Startup** — solo/indie focused

### Tier 4 — Press & extension
19. **SideProjectors** — side-project audience
20. **Toolfolio** — design tools
21. **Microlaunch** — pre-launch teaser

## The 7-Day Blitz Schedule

- **Day 0 (Mon):** submit to Betalist, Uneed (weekly), queue Tuesday PH launch
- **Day 1 (Tue):** 12:01 AM PST → ProductHunt live. Rally your network. Engage comments.
- **Day 2 (Wed):** Show HN + Indie Hackers + Reddit (your top sub). Native posts, not press-release voice.
- **Day 3 (Thu):** OpenAlternative, SaaSHub, AlternativeTo — directory submissions with deep profiles.
- **Day 4 (Fri):** Peerlist, PeerPush, TinyLaunch, Fazier — momentum carry.
- **Day 5 (Sat):** TAAFT, DevHunt, Toolfolio, SaaS Genius — niche directories.
- **Day 6 (Sun):** SideProjectors, Microlaunch, TrustMRR — round-out.
- **Day 7 (Mon):** retro. Numbers, lessons, next bet.

## What the skill autogenerates per platform

For each of the 21 platforms, the skill produces:

1. **Platform-native copy** — e.g., HN is informal and skeptical-welcome, PH wants "maker comment" with behind-the-scenes, Reddit demands value-first-not-pitch
2. **Title variants** — 3 options ranked by predicted CTR
3. **Asset checklist** — what image/video/link each platform requires
4. **Engagement script** — what to say when someone skeptical asks "X" in comments
5. **Cross-link checklist** — when platform A drives traffic to platform B

## Installation (OpenClaw)

1. Save as `SKILL-launch-blitz.md` in:
   ```
   ~/.openclaw/skills/launch-blitz/SKILL.md
   ```

2. Tell your AI:
   > "Install Launch Blitz. Draft a 7-day blitz schedule for launching [PRODUCT] starting [DATE]. Generate native copy for each of the 21 platforms, using my brand voice + bilingual EN/AR."

3. AI outputs a `launch-blitz/` folder with 21 subfolders — one per platform — each containing ready-to-paste copy.

4. On launch days, the AI nudges you in Telegram at the right moment per platform.

## Results from real launches

Felix Craft (Masinov Company) used a similar 21-platform blitz, hit top-5 on ProductHunt, and drove $62K in revenue in 2.5 months from launch. The pattern is not magic — it's compounding: each platform feeds the next.

**What kills launches:** submitting to 1 platform, burning out, forgetting the 20 others.

**What wins:** a scheduled blitz that your AI drives without you having to pump 7 days of energy.
